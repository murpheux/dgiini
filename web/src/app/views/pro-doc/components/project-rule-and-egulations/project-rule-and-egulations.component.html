12. PROJECT RULES AND REGULATIONS
12.1. National personnel
12.1.1 ICAO, as the executing agency for project {{projectCode}} and acting on behalf of the [ENTITY], has been requested by [ENTITY] to hire national personnel in the specialties required for technical assistance.
12.1.2 The national staff appointed by [ENTITY] will depend at all times on the Institution to which it belongs. No labour relations will be established with ICAO, which is the executing agency and acts on behalf of the [ENTITY]. Under no circumstances may ICAO be considered as a joint or substitute employer.
12.1.3 The subscriber should be part of the project team and work under the general direction of [ENTITY], which will coordinate, as necessary, with ICAO in Montreal, which plays a purely administrative role in the context of the project.
12.1.4 The contracting of subscribers must be made through a service contracts that comply with the relevant national regulations in terms of benefits to the subscriber. For the management of local staff expected to provide services annually by reaching the maximum contract period (11 months and 25 days for SSA and 12 months for service contract), the project will ensure transfer of necessary funds to ICAO covering at a minimum six (6) months of the contract period.
12.1.5 Payment for these services will be made by ICAO as facilitator and in compliance with the interest derived from this agreement, and not as a debtor of the contractual obligation between [ENTITY] and the subscriber.
12.2. International experts/personnel
12.2.1 ICAO will recruit and deploy international experts/personnel in accordance with ICAO policies, practices, ICAO/TCB Field Staff Services Rules and applicable process and procedures. In particular, as consultants engaged by ICAO, their entitlement payments will be issued by ICAO. The lead-time required for the recruitment of the experts may range between six (6) weeks to three (3) months, from the moment the funds are received at ICAO until the deployment of the experts. In the eventuality of a contract extension being required for one or more ICAO expert(s), the [ENTITY] will need to issue the necessary request at least three (3) months prior to the end of the ICAO expert(s)’ contract, subject to availability of funds; the request will include a justification and corresponding additional duration, as appropriate.
12.3. Procurement
12.3.1 The procurement of equipment or services are carried out in accordance with ICAO’s Procurement Code, Financial Regulations and Rules, and applicable process and procedures.
12.4. Finance
12.4.1 The reception and management of funds for this project are subject to ICAO’s Financial Regulations and Rules, and applicable process and procedures. The use of any of the resources for this project will be processed upon reception of proper formal authorization from the government or its entitled representative and ICAO.
12.4.2 The unauthorized use of project resources is not allowed and could result in project delay and the discontinuation of the project’s activities.
12.4.3 The provision of financial management and budgetary control of the project and submitting financial reports will be in accordance with ICAO rules, regulations, process and procedures.
12.4.4 ICAO will provide to the [ENTITY] the financial statements concerning the services covered by this document, indicating the status of the funds, in US dollars. Any clarification or discrepancy with the financial statements must be officially communicated to ICAO within 30 days of receiving them, after which they are deemed accepted by the Government.
12.4.5 If additional information is required after the deadlines set in the previous paragraph, costs related to such information will be charged to the project, with prior approval from the State.

12.5. General regulations
12.5.1 All project’s activities are managed in accordance with the applicable ICAO policies, rules, regulations, processes and practices and applicable process and procedures. These will include development of comprehensive work plan and communication strategy.
12.5.2 All project activities, including reports and/or deliverables prepared by the ICAO experts may be reviewed by ICAO Headquarters and/or Regional Office, as appropriate.
12.5.3 This document was developed in English by the parties. Any document related to the implementation of this project that is required by the State to be translated into another official language of ICAO, as may be required, shall be performed by ICAO and charged to the project, as appropriate. Any document related to the implementation of this project that is required by the State to be translated into any other language not an official language of ICAO, shall be translated by the State. In case of disagreements on the text of any of the documents, the prevailing version will be the text in the original language.
